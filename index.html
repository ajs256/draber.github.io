Spelling Bee Assistant

  • Tweet

Spelling Bee Assistant is a JavaScript bookmarklet for Spelling Bee, the New York Times’ popular word puzzle.

It's a great game but if you need the tiniest hint, you are on your own. If you don't make it to the end - and honestly, how often did you you see the Queen Bee screen come up - you'll have to wait to the next day to find out which words you've been missing.

For those of you who play on a laptop or a desktop computer, the Spelling Bee Assistant can offer a bit of support. It comes with a whole range of tools that can help you to succeed without spoiling the fun. Oh, and before we forget: it brings the long promised dark mode to the game!

The installation couldn't be easier: drag and drop the picture at the right into your browser’s bookmark menu and you're done. Next time you are playing, just click on the bookmarklet to display the assistant.

Features

  • Dark Mode

    Have you been staring at the screen for a long time? Your eyes are dry and hurt but you can't stop playing, just this one level! People on social media had suggested to bring Dark Mode to Spelling Bee and the Spelling Bee Assistant does just that. Go to Set-Up, check the Dark Mode box and relax!

  • Score so far

    How far have you been moving ahead already and long is the way to the top? And - and that's a tiny spoiler - how many points will you need to have to become Queen Bee? That's the sort of question you get answered here.

  • Spoilers

    So you found a good bunch of words, discovered the Pangram but now you wonder if there are words with four letters left that you've been missing. Maybe there is another Pangram hidden in the hive? Or at least a good long term that moves you to the next level? The Spoiler panel won't tell you the words, but it will tell you, how many of each size lay still ahead.

  • Spill the beans

    What was the name of this bird again, that you only know from solving crosswords? You know what it starts with, but what came after the 3rd letter again? This little face might know a thing or two that you don't, so watch it while you type!

    • 🙂 a smile means you are on the right track
    • 🙁 a sad face says you're barking up the wrong tree.
    • 😐 no emotion means there is nothing to say; either you didn't type anything yet - or the complete word is already right in front of you.

  • Steps to success

    You’ve certainly seen this one before, which is no wonder, because it's pretty close to the Rankings pop-up that is already part of Spelling Bee. But this one you can keep open while you're playing and - as an added bonus - it reveals how far the road to Queen Bee is. The tier that you’re currently in is highlighted.

  • Surrender

    You aren’t ready to declare defeat already, are you? Well, if you are, no need to wait until 3:00 a.m. ET anymore - with this button you can resolve the game with a mouse clicks. But don't expect to be crowned Queen Bee! All the words you didn’t find will be linked to the respective terms in Google. Maybe this will help you next time!

  • Set-up

    So you like only some of the toys here, but not all of them? Get rid of those you don’t like, they won't bother you anymore. Unless of course you use the assistant on another browser, then you'll have to do it all over again.

  • Little extras

    • You can grab the Spelling Bee Assistant by the header to drag it anywhere inside the game area. Click on the arrow to minimize it and on × to remove it from the game.

    • Pangrams are underlined

What is a bookmarklet?

Bookmarklets are small pieces of code that can enhance the functionality of a website. They reside, just like regular bookmarks, in your browser’s bookmark menu. Unfortunately, there is no straightforward way to assign an icon to them.

Does it work everywhere?

The assistant is not suitable for the mobile devices and has only been tested with the desktop version of the game. It requires a modern browser and won’t work in Internet Explorer.

Does it not spoil the game?

Other people have been creating tools with a similar purpose which - according to this article - the Spelling Bee community seems to like well.

Use the Assistant sparsely, don't launch it before having solved a good chunk of the game on your own. Don't open the Spoilers panel before you really need to. Only hit the magic button when you eventually run completely out of ideas. At least for me it works well this way.
